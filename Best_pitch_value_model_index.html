Methodology
How both models are computed and what data underlies them.

Data source: 4,858 starting pitcher game logs from Baseball Savant, 2024 MLB regular season. SP ERA 4.16, RP ERA 4.10 from Baseball Reference. Linear weights from FanGraphs Guts! (2024).

MODEL 1 — SINGLE-GAME EXPECTED RUNS

At each pitch count N, compute expected innings remaining from the starter using the survival curve (probability starter is still in at pitch n, summed forward and divided by 16.4 pitches/inning). Reliever innings = total remaining innings minus starter innings. Expected runs = SP_IP × (SP_ERA/9) + RP_IP × (RP_ERA/9). PA value = reduction in expected runs from pitch S to pitch S+L.

SP_IP_rem(N) = Σ survival(n)/survival(N) for n = N+1..115, ÷ 16.4
RP_IP_rem(N) = (9 − N/16.4) − SP_IP_rem(N)
E[runs](N) = SP_IP_rem × SP_ERA/9 + RP_IP_rem × RP_ERA/9
PA_value(S, L) = E[runs](S) − E[runs](S+L)
MODEL 2 — SERIES FATIGUE MULTIPLIER

From Bradbury & Forman (2012, J Strength Cond Res 26(5):1181–7): each pitch thrown in the preceding game increases ERA by 0.007 in the next game. Applied to relievers with a 0.8 scaling factor. Extra RP pitches burned today degrade RP ERA for the remaining series games, applied to ~3.8 RP innings/game × (series_length − 1) games.

extra_rp_pitches = ΔPitcher_count × 1.0 (approx)
ERA_penalty = extra_rp_pitches × 0.007 × recovery_scale
carry_over_runs = ERA_penalty × rp_innings_remaining_series / 9
series_multiplier = (base_value + carry_over) / base_value

Source: Bradbury JC, Forman SL. The impact of pitch counts and days of rest on performance among major-league baseball pitchers. J Strength Cond Res. 2012;26(5):1181-1187.

RAW vs WOBA-SCALED LINEAR WEIGHTS

FanGraphs publishes wOBA-scaled weights (single = 0.882, HR = 2.050) which are multiplied by ~1.24 to align wOBA with OBP scale. These are NOT true run values. Raw linear weights (single = 0.47, HR = 1.40) represent the actual expected run change per outcome and are the correct basis for comparing depletion value.
